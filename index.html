Our app's aim is to guide new students, new staff or visitors and help them learn their way around campus....

We have just started this project and are currently working along with stakeholders to decide our functional and non-functional requirements as well as how the app itself will look like.

Faeremin

Touchless instrument inspired by the electronic musical instrument, the theremin.

In order to play the theremin, you require incredible body control. Our prototype, the "Faeremin", with its simplified design, accommodates people who lack those motor skills.... For instance, people with Parkinson's disease, dyspraxia or in early stages of motor beuron diseases would benefit from our solution as well as amputees or people born with amelia due to its simplified desgin.

Please watch the video on the left to see a demo of how the instrument is played.

This setup includes:

 2 Arduino UNO
 2 Liquid Crystal Display Screens 16 x 2
 2 Ultrasonic Distance Sensors
 2 Speakers
 3 10 kΩ resistors
 2 9V Batteries
 2 Transistors
 1 piezo buzzer
 4 250 kΩ potentiometer  3 buttons

Whac-A-Mole

Arduino UNO LED Whack-A-Mole game.

This was my first ever Arduino project, a modification of the game "Whac-A-Mole", a 2-player reflex game where the aim is to press a button when any of your colour LEDs lights up.... If you manage to press the button in time, you will score a point. The first player to reach 10 points will be the winner and the game will end.

Please watch the video on the right to see a demo of how the game works.

This Arduino UNO setup includes:

 1 Liquid Crystal Display Screen 16 x 2
 1 shift register
 10 LEDs
 3 10k Ω resistors
 10 560 Ω resistors
 1 servo motor
 3 buttons
 1 piezo buzzer
 1 250 kΩ potentiometer
